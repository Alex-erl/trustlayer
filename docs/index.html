Autonomous agents move money, sign, and decide on people's behalf. TrustLayer turns every agent action into a tamper-evident evidence bundle — bound to the agent's identity and authority, witnessed by independent sources, and verifiable offline by anyone. No database to trust. No vendor lock-in. Just cryptography you can re-check yourself.

      The pipeline
      
        
      Identity and authority in. A self-provable record out.
      
        
      Every step uses open standards and deterministic cryptography, so the final bundle can be reconstructed and checked by anyone, anywhere — no access to the service required.
      
      
      
      
      
        
      01
      Identity & mandate
      The agent gets a did:key and a signed, scoped authorization.
      did:key · W3C VC · mandate
      
        
      02
      Agent acts
      The action and its payload are recorded as an immutable event.
      event + payload
      
        
      03
      Hash
      SHA-256 over canonical JSON — deterministic across machines.
      canonical · SHA-256
      
        
      04
      Multi-anchor
      The hash is witnessed by several independent media at once.
      notary · log · RFC 3161 · ledger
      
        
      05
      Seal bundle
      Everything is packaged and sealed with a bundle hash.
      evidence bundle
      
        
      06
      Verify offline
      Anyone re-checks integrity, identity, authority — with no server.
      any language · keyless
      
      
      
    
      
  
      

  
      
    
      
      
      
        
      Why it matters
      
        
      A human-centric trust primitive for the agent economy.
      
      
      
      
      
        
      🔍
      Verify, don't trust
      Verification is public, keyless, and offline. Auditors and regulators re-check the math themselves — the operator can't fake, edit, or back-date a record.
      
        
      🧩
      Open by design
      Open format spec, Apache-2.0 verifiers, self-hostable server. Bundles are plain JSON — you are never locked to one vendor to read your own evidence.
      
        
      🇪🇺
      Built on EU standards
      did:key / W3C VC, eIDAS 2 & EUDI-aligned, RFC 3161/6962 witnessing — mapped to EU AI Act Art. 12 record-keeping duties.
      
        
      ⚓
      Independent witnesses
      A record is anchored into several sources at once. It stays provable as long as any one survives, and stronger when several independently agree.
